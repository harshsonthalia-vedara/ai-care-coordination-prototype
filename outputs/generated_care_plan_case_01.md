**Patient Summary**  
Maria Johnson is a 57-year-old female with a history of Type 2 Diabetes and Hypertension, currently at high risk. She reports persistent fatigue, poor medication adherence (specifically with Metformin), and inconsistent blood sugar monitoring. Maria has poor glycemic control with a recent HbA1c value of 9.2%. She missed a specialist appointment due to lack of transportation and has difficulty maintaining a healthy diet due to financial constraints and limited food access.

---

**Identified Care Gaps**
- Poor diabetes control (HbA1c 9.2%)
- Poor medication adherence, especially Metformin
- Inconsistent self-monitoring of blood glucose
- Missed specialist appointment due to transportation barriers
- Symptoms of fatigue and dizziness
- Limited access to healthy food (food insecurity)
- Moderate adherence to antihypertensive therapy (Lisinopril)

---

**Recommended Interventions**

**High Priority**
- Address poor glycemic control: Coordinate urgent follow-up with diabetes care provider given HbA1c of 9.2% and recent claims for hyperglycemia and dizziness.
- Facilitate transportation: Arrange reliable transportation to ensure Maria can attend upcoming medical appointments.
- Improve medication adherence: Provide pill organizer and set up medication reminders; assess for barriers such as cost or understanding.
- Address food insecurity: Refer to local food assistance programs or diabetes-friendly community resources.

**Moderate Priority**
- Support blood sugar self-monitoring: Educate on the importance of regular glucose checks and provide access to glucometer supplies if needed.
- Monitor hypertension management: Ensure prescription refills and reinforce adherence to Lisinopril.
- Provide education about symptoms: Teach Maria about the warning signs of hyper- and hypoglycemia and when to seek help.

**Low Priority**
- Explore behavioral health needs: Assess for underlying depression or cognitive issues contributing to fatigue and adherence challenges at a future visit.
- Long-term: Ongoing nutrition education and diabetes self-management support.

---

**Follow-Up Actions**
- Schedule urgent provider appointment for diabetes management (coordinate transportation in advance).
- Contact Maria within one week to confirm medication adherence and appointment status.
- Refer to community resources for food assistance and nutrition counseling.
- Arrange for a community health worker or case manager to provide ongoing support with care coordination.
- Reassess hypertension management and address any additional barriers at next clinical interaction.
- Document interventions and monitor progress in care management system.
